ICLAB Interative Data Display not just a map -- it's a snapshot of the global internet censorship sphere. This map represents statistical information in a graphic representation of how governments disproportionately monitor peoples worldwide. It was made by Emma Wolff, an undergraduate student studying International Relations, Computer Science, and Arabic at Mount Holyoke College. This project is a final project for Smith College internet censorship course, with Professor Shinyoung Cho, a co-reseacher on the ICLab project.

* * *

What is ICLAB ?
ICLAB is a global, longitudinal, internet censorship measurement platform launched by academic researchers [Arian Akhavan Niaki, Shinyoung Cho, Zachary Weinberg, Nguyen Phong Hoang - University of Massachusetts, Amherst Abbas Razaghpanah, Nicolas Christin - Stony Brook University Phillipa Gill - Carnegie Mellon University] to gain a holistic understanding of global internet censorship.
Collecting censorship data for a comprehensive, global, longitudinal perspective remains challenging, thus this wasn't an easy task.
Here are some fast specs about the project:
Operated continuously since late 2016
Use commercial VPNs as vantage points
Records and archives raw data, additional research possible
Within 53,906,532 measurements of individual web pages, collected by ICLab in 2017 and 2018, we observe blocking of 3,602 unique URLs in 60 countries

* * *

62 countries were observed by the ICLAB team in 2017. These countries broadly fall into three categories based on Freedom House data: Free, Partly Free, and Not Free. Free is reflected as green, partly free is reflected as blue, and not free is reflected as red. All other countries are monotone. The Stars are used to display VOD by continent. These are not country specifc but rather for the respective region.
